General Instructions

In this experiment, your task will be to read a word printed on the screen and respond by typing aditional words. You will do so with your computer keyboard. It is important that you are sitting in your normal typing position and that you type as you normally would as if you were taking notes or writing in a Word document.

Take the opportunity to ready yourself now for typing. You will practice typing before any of the real tasks start.

There will be 9 trials totalwith each trial lasting 2 minutes except the first trial, which is a practice trial and will last 30 seconds. You will be allowed to take a break between every trial, but remember, you have 60 minutes to complete this HIT. The tasks themselves will take no more than 18 minutes, including the short questionnaire at the end.

Here is the flow of the experiment:

  1. 1 practice trial
  2. 8 experiment trials
  3. 1 short demographic questionnaire

Task Instructions

Great job practicing. The real experimental trials start now!

There will be Eight 2-minute experimental trials (~16 minutes). You can take short breaks in between each of the trials, but make sure you don't take too long a break so that you can finish in time.

Your Task:

A word will appear on the screen, and your task is to type other words that are creative, related to that word.

It is very important that you only type SINGLE WORDS, that is, DO NOT type sentences or phrases.

Each word will appear on the screen for 2 minutes. It is important to try and think of responses the whole time, though we do want you to think of creative responses. Just try to keep thinking for the whole 2 minutes.

Remember to type your ideas as soon as you think of them. Then, press ENTER after EACH response

When you are read, press START below to begin the first real trial.
